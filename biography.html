Biography

José Rizal

José Rizal (1861–1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary who played a crucial role in the movement for Philippine independence from Spanish rule. Born in Calamba, Laguna, he was a polymath skilled in various fields, including medicine, literature, and the arts. He studied in the University of Santo Tomas and later in Europe, where he earned a degree in ophthalmology.

Rizal's most famous works, Noli Me Tangere (1887) and El Filibusterismo (1891), exposed the injustices of Spanish colonial rule and inspired the Philippine Revolution. Due to his writings and activism, he was arrested, exiled to Dapitan, and later executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, in Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park, Manila). His martyrdom solidified his status as the Philippine national hero and a symbol of the country's fight for freedom.
