Introduction

Noli Me Tangere is a novel by Filipino writer and national hero José Rizal, first published in 1887. Written in Spanish, it critiques the abuses of Spanish colonial rule and the Catholic clergy in the Philippines. The story follows Crisostomo Ibarra, a young Filipino who returns home after studying in Europe, only to face corruption, injustice, and oppression.

Biography

José Rizal (1861–1896) was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary. Born in Calamba, Laguna, he was a polymath skilled in medicine, literature, and the arts. His most famous works, Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, exposed the injustices of Spanish colonial rule and inspired the Philippine Revolution. He was executed in 1896, becoming a symbol of the country's fight for freedom.

Works

During the 19th century, the Philippines was under Spanish colonial rule. Rizal's Noli Me Tangere highlights the oppression and suffering of Filipinos. The novel was banned by Spanish authorities but spread throughout the country, inspiring nationalism and the Philippine Revolution.

Plot Summary

The novel follows Crisostomo Ibarra, who returns to the Philippines after studying in Europe. He faces corruption and betrayal, particularly from the Spanish friars. The story explores themes of injustice, love, and the struggle for freedom.

Key Characters

  • Crisóstomo Ibarra - The protagonist, a young idealist.
  • María Clara - Ibarra's love interest, symbolizing the oppressed Filipina.
  • Padre Dámaso - A corrupt friar and antagonist.
  • Elias - A revolutionary who helps Ibarra.

Analysis

The themes of Noli Me Tangere—social injustice, political corruption, and the struggle for freedom—remain relevant today. The novel encourages critical thinking and social action, inspiring readers to challenge oppressive systems and advocate for reform.
